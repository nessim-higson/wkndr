I.
The Muiderslot, twenty-five minutes from Amsterdam, goes medieval for two days only this weekend.
I. Today + Mon Only For the kids

A medieval day at the Muiderslot↗

Muiden · 25 minutes by train · 10:30 → 16:30

The castle goes fully medieval for two days only — sword-fighting school for kids, fire shows, troubadours, knightings in the courtyard. Your four-year-old can get knighted at the Bataille Knight School and make their own knight creation in the workshop. The grounds are big enough to wander all day, with water on three sides and shade for the worst of the heat.

Why now Two-day-only event · knighting works from age four · castle rewards a sunny day.

II.
The Fluistervink, an electric whisper-boat, returned to the Bos this week for its summer season.
II. Season Just Opened For the kids

The Fluistervink silent boat↗

Amsterdamse Bos · 25 min by bike · hourly departures

An electric whisper-boat through the forest — no engine noise, just water, birds, the occasional deer at the shore. It is, plainly, one of the most peaceful hours the city can offer. The variety of small things to look at keeps a four-year-old fixed to the window. Just opened for the season this week.

Why now First weekend of the season · forest stays cool while city warms · sustains a child's attention.

III.
The Pannenkoekenboot — the all-you-can-eat Amstel cruise, the Sunday family default.
III. For the kids Default

Vondelpark, then pancakes on the Amstel↗

Vondelpark + Pannenkoekenboot · 15 min by bike

The classic Amsterdam Sunday: bike Vondelpark, release the child at Groot Melkhuis, then ferry to the Pannenkoekenboot for lunch on the water. The boat does a 75-min loop down the Amstel while you eat all-you-can-eat pancakes. Kids get windows full of boats and bridges; the boat is the adventure. IJscuypje on the way home if earned.

Why now Park weather · the boat contains the child · pancakes are the bribe.
