Weekend of 23–24 May 2026 · written for Ness, partner, kids

The year's first hot Saturday hits, and the city is already outside.

This is a terrace-and-water weekend, not a museum weekend. Two things shape what's worth your time: the weather (30°, no rain, the kind of day Amsterdam waits eight months for), and that you've got the kids both days. Below: three picks worth getting on the calendar, then a deeper list if those don't fit, and one escape if you'd rather flip the whole thing.

№ 01 / SAT MORNING → AFTERNOON
Unsplash
Weather pick Kids Outdoors · Free swim

Hortus Botanicus, then a swim at Marineterrein

Plantage Middenlaan · Kattenburgerstraat · 20 min between on bikes

Hortus opens at 10. The butterfly greenhouse is the part the kids will remember for a week — go straight there, then loop through the medicinal garden while they're still patient. By 12:30 they'll be done. Bike north to Marineterrein, where the harbor swim opens this weekend for the season. Bring towels and the bucket. It's the closest thing the city has to a beach moment without driving to IJburg.

You've saved three Hortus posts on Instagram since February and never been with them. Marineterrein opens for swimming this exact weekend — first chance of the year. Both free or near it.
№ 02 / SAT 20:30 · PARADISO
Unsplash
Sold out · waitlist Critic's pick Live music

Nectar Woode + Arno Sacco

Paradiso Upstairs · Weteringschans 6–8 · in collab with NOTION

Ghanaian-British jazz-soul singer, the kind of voice that makes a room hold its breath. Booked into the small hall, which is the point — she'll be three meters from you. NOTION programming means the warm-up will be worth showing up early for. Sold out, but the waitlist usually moves: enable notifications and check at 19:00.

If you can't get in: KC Lights is at Thuishaven Sunday afternoon, day-into-night, softer landing for a hot weekend than a club Saturday anyway.

Your top Spotify weeks all skew voice-forward soul (Cleo Sol, Snoh Aalegra, Kelela). Nectar Woode sits exactly in that pocket. Paradiso Upstairs is where you spent two Saturdays in March.
№ 03 / SUN LUNCH · NOORD
Unsplash
New · Opened March Eater Hit List Terrace

Papillon

Amsterdam Noord · all-day · walk-in lunch, book for dinner

The all-day place in Noord everyone's been talking about since spring. Sourdough and pastries in the morning, wood-fired plates and natural wines as the day shifts. The terrace is the reason to go this Sunday — south-facing, properly sunny, glass of something cold, kids can roam. Cross on the ferry from Centraal, make a morning of it.

You've never been and it's been featured by three writers you follow this spring (Eater, We Like Amsterdam, Your Little Black Book). Sunday brunch + a real terrace fits the weather perfectly.

↳ If you want to flip the script

One escape: Texel, two days, ferry from Den Helder

The first heat of the year is also the first weekend the Wadden makes sense again. Train to Den Helder 90 min, ferry 20. The kids will lose their minds on the beach. Boutique stays in Den Burg still had weekend rooms at 21:00 — three within budget. Back Sunday evening, no work day lost.
