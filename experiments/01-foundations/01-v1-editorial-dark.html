Brief №27 · for the weekend of 23–24 May 2026

The year's first hot Saturday hits, and the city is already outside.

Read in: this is a terrace-and-water weekend, not a museum weekend. Two things shape what's worth your time: the weather (30°, no rain, the kind of day Amsterdam waits eight months for), and that you've got the 4-year-old both days. Below: one move per slot — Saturday morning with the kid, Saturday night for you and her after bedtime, Sunday slow. One escape option if you want to flip the script entirely. Tulip season's over; berry-and-festival season starts now.

№1
Weather pick Kid-friendly Outdoors Sat morning → afternoon

Hortus Botanicus, then a swim at Marineterrein

Plantage Middenlaan·Kattenburgerstraat·20 min on bikes between them

Hortus opens at 10. The butterfly greenhouse is the part a 4-year-old will remember for a week — go straight there, then loop through the medicinal garden while she's still patient. By 12:30 she'll be done. Bike north to Marineterrein, where the harbor swim opens this weekend for the season. Bring a towel and the bucket. It's the closest thing the city has to a real beach moment without the IJburg drive.

Why this fits You've saved 3 Hortus posts on Instagram since February and never been with her. Marineterrein opens for swimming this exact weekend — first chance of the year. Both are free or near-free.
№2
Sold out · waitlist Critic's pick Live music Sat 20:30 — Paradiso

Nectar Woode + Arno Sacco

Paradiso Upstairs·Weteringschans 6–8·in collab with NOTION

Ghanaian-British jazz-soul singer, the kind of voice that makes a room hold its breath. Booked into the small hall, which is the point — she'll be three meters from you. NOTION programming means the warm-up will be worth showing up early for. Sold out, but the waitlist usually moves: enable notifications and check at 19:00.

If you can't get in: KC Lights is at Thuishaven Sunday for an outdoor day-into-night thing, which is a softer landing for a hot Sunday than a sweaty club Saturday anyway.

Why this fits Your top Spotify weeks all skew toward voice-forward soul (Cleo Sol, Snoh Aalegra, Kelela). Nectar Woode sits exactly in that pocket. Paradiso Upstairs is the room where you spent two Saturdays in March.
№3
New Eater Hit List Terrace Sun lunch — Noord

Papillon

Amsterdam Noord·opened March·walk-in lunch, book for dinner

The all-day place in Noord everyone's been talking about since spring. Sourdough and pastries in the morning, wood-fired plates and natural wines as the day shifts. The terrace is the reason to go this Sunday — south-facing, properly sunny, glass of something cold, kid can roam. Cross on the ferry from Centraal, make a morning of it.

Why this fits You've never been to Papillon and it's been on three of the writers you follow (Eater, We Like Amsterdam, Your Little Black Book). Sunday brunch with the kid + a real terrace fits the weather signal perfectly.

One escape: Texel, two days, ferry from Den Helder

The first heat of the year is also the first weekend the Wadden makes sense again. Train to Den Helder is 90 min, ferry 20. The 4-year-old will lose her mind on the beach. Boutique stays in Den Burg still have weekend rooms (checked at 21:00, 3 within budget). Back Sunday evening, no work day lost.
