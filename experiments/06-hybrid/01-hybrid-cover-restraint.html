Medieval Days at Muiderslot Castle
Muiderslot, Muiden · outdoor castle grounds · €18 adults · €12 kids

The castle goes full medieval this weekend — sword fighting school for kids, fire shows, troubadours, battles in the courtyard. Your four-year-old can get knighted at the Bataille Knight School and make their own knight creation in the castle workshop. The grounds are big enough to wander all day and the lakeside setting means there's water, picnic spots, and shade when needed. Real moat, real towers, dragons not included.

Fluistervink silent boat through Amsterdamse Bos
Amsterdamse Bos · covered boat through forest waterways · €14 adults · €9 kids

Electric whisper-boat through the forest — no engine noise, just water, birds, the occasional deer at the shore. It's one of the most peaceful hours you can buy in Amsterdam, and the kind of thing a four-year-old will actually sit still for because there's so much to watch. Just opened for the season this week. Bring snacks; trips run about an hour. After, hit the rowing lake for ice cream or rent a kayak at Bosbaan if there's energy left.

SUNDAY LAZY MODE · 15 MIN BY BIKE
Vondelpark loop, then pancakes on the Amstel
Vondelpark + Pannenkoekenboot · bike-able loop · ~€30 family lunch

The classic Amsterdam Sunday: bike Vondelpark, let the four-year-old loose at the Groot Melkhuis playground, then ferry over to the Pannenkoekenboot for lunch on the water. The boat does a 75-minute Amstel loop while you eat all-you-can-eat pancakes — kids get to look out the windows at the boats and bridges and the boat itself feels like the adventure. IJscuypje at the Apollolaan on the way home if the kid earned it.
