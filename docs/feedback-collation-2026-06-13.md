# WKNDR — feedback collation (first link recipients, 2026-06-13)

_Reactions from friends Ness shared the live link with (WhatsApp). Read through the
Mom Test lens: these are supportive friends reacting to the IDEA, not strangers who
round-tripped a link to plan a real weekend. Most of it is the compliment/feature-idea
zone — useful for direction, weak as demand evidence. The behavioral round-trip test
(docs/validation-log.md) is still open. One finding here IS hard behavioral data; it's
listed first._

## 1. THE hard signal (behavioral, not opinion)

**The recipient could not tell what WKNDR is.** First message, unprompted:
> "I need a bit more info? It's missing an about section so I'm unclear? A weekend
> events guide? You pick your event and then match with people going and ask them to
> join?"

Two facts in that one message:
- **The product does not explain itself on open.** This is the cold-open failure the
  office-hours review flagged (the recipient's first 60 seconds), now confirmed in the
  wild. It gates everything — a share link that needs a paragraph of WhatsApp
  explanation can't spread on its own.
- **Their default mental model was "meet strangers going to the event,"** NOT "align
  with people I already know." The actual thesis (kill the "what should we do?" loop
  with your partner/group) did not land at all. People pattern-match WKNDR to
  Meetup/social-discovery before they pattern-match it to couples-planning.

## 2. The three-product fork (where the friends pulled)

The feedback keeps splitting WKNDR into three different products. Naming them so we
choose on purpose instead of drifting:

| | What it is | Job | Friend signal | Cold-start cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — The Guide** | A discerning, weather-ranked, weekly-fresh Amsterdam what's-on | discovery | "a much needed guide to Amsterdam like Time Out but that doesn't exist… I think people want this" | **low** — one person, instant value |
| **B — The Alignment tool** | swipe-to-match so a couple/group converges on a plan | agreement (our validated wedge) | "interesting from an alignment purpose… it's analysis paralysis" | **high** — needs BOTH people to get it cold |
| **C — The Social network** | meet new *curated* interesting people | meet-people | "filter the people I ask to go with… so you don't end up with people that lack taste or intelligence" + The Breakfast ref | high — two-sided marketplace |

Both friends pulled hardest toward **C** (the people are the interesting variable to
them), citing **The Breakfast** as the model: members-only offline social network,
~62k members × €11/mo. That's a real local proof that curated-social monetizes — but
it's a different job with a different cold-start, not WKNDR's validated direction.

## 3. Convergent MVP advice (independent of our strategy work)

The friend, with zero knowledge of the office-hours/JTBD docs, arrived at the same place:
> "Just start with one mechanic. First is just the events alone. MVP babes. Set
> yourself the challenge: simplest version you can launch in 30 days, then see what
> comes back. This app will spread very quickly by word of mouth and network sharing."

Note the friend's sequencing: **events/guide FIRST, matching LATER.** This is a vote
for A-as-wedge — and it's a real correction to our earlier "matching is the wedge"
call (see §5 of the response / office-hours update). The reason it's right: matching
(B) requires the *recipient* to understand the product cold, and §1 shows recipients
don't. The guide (A) needs no second person and no comprehension. So A is the wedge;
sharing is the growth layer; matching is the moat layer you earn after traction.

## 4. Concrete requests (cheap, do-able)

- **Categories:** "talks & lectures" (×2), crafts (ceramics workshops), sports / run
  clubs, museum nights. Note the pattern — these all skew **experiential /
  participatory**, not passive consumption. A hint about what "discerning" means for
  this audience.
- **About / framing line on open** — the #1 fix from §1.

## 5. Revenue ideas raised (PARKED — validate want first)

Ness held the right line in-thread ("I need to see if the idea sticks and people want
it — that's problem no. 1"). Logged, not acted on:
- Monthly/yearly subscription
- Venues/events pay a listing fee
- Curated brand suggestions to the audience
- Proof point: The Breakfast ≈ €11/mo × ~62k members (curated-local-Amsterdam can pay)

## 6. Mom Test reality check (so we don't fool ourselves)

"LAUNCH", "people want this", "new expats would eat this up", "it'll spread by word of
mouth" — this is the **supportive-friend zone**. It feels like validation; it is not
demand. Nobody in these threads used WKNDR to plan an actual weekend or round-tripped a
link. Weight it as direction (positioning, categories, the comprehension bug), not as
proof. The real test — strangers round-tripping a link, a plan getting lived — is still
ahead in docs/validation-log.md.

## 7. What changes

1. **Build the one-line "about" / self-explaining open.** Highest leverage, gates the
   share loop. (#1 finding.)
2. **Reframe the MVP to the single-player Guide (A).** Sharing on top; matching parked
   until traction. Collapse the app surface to that one legible path.
3. **Hold the line** against pivoting into The Breakfast (C) or chasing "expats" as a
   new market — note them, don't chase (Ness's lathe: tweak one variable, don't
   new-market-hunt).
4. **Add experiential categories** (talks/lectures, workshops, run clubs, museum
   nights) when touching content — cheap, on-signal.
